The Reality of AI

Artificial intelligence is not weightless. It runs on power, water, chips, cooling, land, and public infrastructure.

The Green Code is a framework for making sure AI systems are judged not only by their capabilities, but by whether they create measurable value for society and the planet relative to the resources they consume.

We call this symbiotic AI: accountable, efficient, auditable, and aligned with human sovereignty.

Energy & Resources

AI systems depend on electricity, cooling, water, semiconductor supply chains, and data center infrastructure.

Accountability Gap

Current governance fails to require comprehensive disclosure of environmental burden and net societal return.

The Central Principle

No system this resource-intensive should scale without proving
it contributes more than it consumes.

01

Ecological Accountability

Measure the real cost of AI systems in terms of energy, water, and material resources.

02

Net Public Benefit

Prove that AI creates verified value beyond private extraction and profit.

03

Efficiency by Design

Use the smallest sufficient compute for the task. Optimize before scaling.

04

Human Sovereignty

Protect rights, dignity, consent, and due process under all circumstances.

05

Auditability

If a claim cannot be verified, it should not be treated as compliance.
